Project Summary

Hagley parkrun — Experience and Connection

This project investigates how participation in Hagley parkrun contributes to individual wellbeing and community connectedness. Drawing on relevant literature, theoretical frameworks, and participant interviews, the research explores the factors that motivate individuals to return regularly. Beyond the physical benefits associated with running or walking 5km, the study examines the social rituals, sense of belonging, and shared identity that emerge among volunteers, walkers, and runners. The findings will support the development of an evidence-based engagement strategy for Hagley parkrun, New Zealand's largest parkrun event.
